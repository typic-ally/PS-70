<br> #### Week 2 - 2D Design & Cutting By: Allison Fairman I tried to to construct a laser cutting piece for this week's project using both Fusion 360 and Blender. While I am familiar with blender, I have never used it to make sketches. I had planned to build a house, but because it was difficult to figure out the required measurements for the roof, so I chose to create a puzzle piece instead. <img src="lasercut example base.png" alt="lasercut base" width="600" height="275"> For this puzzle piece, I sketched the sides out using the SOLID line tool. (Here's the base of the puzzle piece before adding the dimensions). <img src="lasercut example 2.png" alt="lasercut example pt 2" width="600" height="275"> <img src="lasercut example 1.png" alt="lasercut example pt 1" width="600" height="230"> Then I changed the dimensions to 100.00-120.00 (using the dimensions tool), and made the cut dimensions the same size as the thickness of the cardboard I used to cut it in. Lastly, I saved the project as a DXF by right-clicking the "sketches" menu under "Browser," then transferred the DXF file to the flash drive. <img src="Lasercut 1.png" alt="lasercut result pt 1" width="499" height="601"> <img src="Lasercut 2.png" alt="lasercut result pt 2" width="499" height="501"> <img src="Lasercut 3.png" alt="lasercut result pt 3" width="499" height="498"> I used a different platform for the 3D design, since Fusion 360 was just too difficult to work with and didn't click for me as a beginner. I'm sure I could spend more time figuring out the platform later, but since this third assignment was time-sensitive, I went with something I could work with. <img src="step 1.png" alt="step 1" width="205" height="239"> <img src="step 2.png" alt="step 2" width="205" height="239"> <img src="step 3.png" alt="step 3" width="180" height="250"> <img src="step 4.png" alt="step 4" width="305" height="250"> <img src="step 5.png" alt="step 5" width="225" height="239"> <img src="step 6.png" alt="step 6" width="225" height="239"> <img src="step 7.png" alt="step 7" width="205" height="239"> I started off the ice cream cone with the base, which is the cone. I went to the 3D shapes menu and selected the "cone" option, then I rotated it vertically by 40 degrees. <img src="step 8.png" alt="step 8" width="488" height="638"> <img src="step 9.png" alt="step 9" width="488" height="638"> <img src="step 10.png" alt="step 10" width="328" height="742"> <img src="step 11.png" alt="step 11" width="328" height="742">\ Next I used the Sphere and Hemisphere to make an ice cream scoop structure. I ended up stacking the Sphere by copying and pasting it and scaling portions of it down to give it a collapsing look. <img src="step 12.png" alt="step 12" width="183" height="273"> <img src="step 13.png" alt="step 13" width="273" height="273"> <img src="step 14.png" alt="step 14" width="120" height="273"> Lastly, I colored each shape and added the whip cream using the 3D "doodle" tool, followed up with the cherry piece by using another sphere and curved cylinder. <img src="step 6.png" alt="sphere" width="256" height="285"> <img src="cherry on top.png" alt="cherry" width="325" height="285"> <img src="step 15.png" alt="step 15" width="585" height="885"> Here is the finishing product!
