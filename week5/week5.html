<br> #### Week 5 - 3D Design & Printing By: Allison Fairman For my 3D design & printing project, I chose to print the Eiffel Tower. I uploaded a stl file of an eiffel tower design that I found online, and put it into Fusion 360. <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-23 110632.png" width="537" height="877"> Then, I extruded it, saved it as a mesh, and exported the file to Prucaslicer using its default settings. <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-25 151541.png" width="1392" height="892"> <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-25 151556.png" width="1086" height="686"> In prucaslicer, I used the "Scale" tool and dragged the orange block to scale it down to a detectable size on the prucaslicer plate (until the mesh turned green). <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 125019.png" width="677" height="417"> <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 125028.png" width="677" height="421"> <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 125146.png" width="677" height="471"> Then, I selected "supports on build plate only," and for the "Infill" section, I chose the "15%" option. After that process, I sliced the mesh, exported the G-code to an SD card, and processed it through the 3D printer. <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-28 054840.png" width="1377" height="477"> Here is the finishing product! <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 144537.png" width="492" height="1276"> <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 144543.png" width="484" height="1225"> <img src="Screenshot 2023-07-27 144551.png" width="487" height="1163"> Source for the Eiffel Tower: STL file: <a download href='./eiffel_final.stl'>Download my STL file <\a> Michal Fanta's 3D design: <a href='Eiffel Tower by Michal Fanta _ Download free STL model _ Printables.com.html'> Eiffel Tower </a>
